The following improvements have been made in the digital version of the Grade 6 Imagine IM curriculum and will be reflected in the next edition of the print materials:

Component Improvement in Digital Date Updated
Section B Checkpoint The Responding to Student Thinking supports have been updated in the Teacher Guide, the Checkpoint Answer Key and in the digital Checkpoint. 6/5/2024
Section C Checkpoint The Responding to Student Thinking supports have been added in the Teacher Guide, the Checkpoint Answer Key and in the digital Checkpoint. 6/5/2024
Lesson 1, Practice Problems The answer to Problem 4 was corrected. 8/30/2024
Lesson 4 In Activity 2, parallelogram A has been updated so both the left and right sides have a length of 5 units. 8/28/2024
Teacher Guide, Section A, Lesson 3, Activity 1, pg. 61 In the students response for part A, the correct answer is 24. (2×6)+(4×3)=24(2×6)+(4×3)=24 8/19/2025
Teacher Guide, Section B, Lesson 5, Warm-up, Student Narrative There is a typo in the last question.  It should read: "How are they different?" 8/19/2025
Teacher Guide, Section B, Lesson 4, Lesson Narrative: A note about notation, pg. 73 The ⋅⋅  and ×× symbols are missing from the second to the last sentence in the Lesson Narrative (a note about notation). It should read "Explain that the ⋅⋅ symbol and the ×× symbol both represent multiplication." 8/19/2025
Teacher Guide, Section B, Lesson 6, Goals, pg. 102 The lesson goal "Apply the formula for the area of a parallelogram..." is repeated. 8/19/2025
Teacher Guide, Section C, Lesson 8, Activity 1, pg. 141 For clarity, one of the strategies to monitor different ways of reasoning has been updated.  
"For Triangle C, some students may choose to draw rectangles around
and on the triangle (as shown here), find half of the area of the largest rectangle, half of the area of the smallest rectangle, and subtract the small area from the large area."		 8/19/2025
Teacher Guide, Section D, Lesson 16, Practice Problem 3, pg. 278 The current answer for A is wrong, therefore B will change as well. 
3a. A and E
3b. A and B (if the volume is presumed to be 5 cubic units),  or B and C, B and D, or C and D (if the volume is presumed to be 6 cubic units)		 8/19/2025
Teacher Guide, Unit-at-a-Glance, Section C Learning Goals, pg. 6 The Learning Goals for Section C have been updated for the third bullet to include the equation 12⋅b⋅h12⋅b⋅h.
  • Understand why the process of finding the area of a triangle can be abstracted as 12⋅b⋅h12⋅b⋅h (or equivalent) and apply the formula to find the area of a triangle.
 8/19/2025
